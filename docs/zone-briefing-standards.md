# Zone Briefing Standards

## Goal
Define what makes a zone brief feel operationally credible without crossing into restricted collection.

## Standard Elements
- One-page executive summary.
- Source-health section with current, due-now, overdue, blocked, and unknown counts.
- Baseline event section with dates, geography, and source IDs.
- Observation section with capture method and source links.
- Anomaly section with score, label, confounds, and next action.
- Claim register with publication label and reviewer state.
- Review decision with decision label, rationale, and follow-up owner.

## Formatting Rules
- Keep sentences short and factual.
- Use UTC timestamps.
- Prefer tables or bullet rows over prose blocks when listing evidence.
- Cite the source ledger snapshot in the header.

## Briefing Discipline
- Lead with the assessment label.
- State the strongest support first, then the limitations.
- Do not bury uncertainty.
- Keep control districts visible whenever a directional change is claimed.

## Acceptance Check
- If the brief cannot be reconstructed from the source ledger and observation log, it is not briefing-grade.
